## What is IPTV?

**IPTV** stands for **Internet Protocol Television**. Instead of signals arriving through a traditional aerial, satellite dish, or cable box alone, IPTV-style services deliver live channels, catch-up, and on-demand libraries over your **home broadband**.

You typically install a supported app on a **Fire TV Stick**, **Smart TV**, **Android box**, **phone**, or **tablet**, then sign in with the details your provider gives you (playlist, portal URL, username/password—depending on the product).

You do **not** need to be technical to use it day to day. You do need to be careful **who you buy from**, because not every advert that promises “all channels” is honest about rights, stability, or refunds.

If you are brand new to the idea, think of IPTV as **“TV apps powered by the internet”**—similar to how Netflix or iPlayer use your broadband, except many IPTV products focus on **live linear channels** and **EPG grids** that feel closer to traditional TV.

### If something goes wrong on day one

**Do not panic.** Nine times out of ten, “it will not connect” is one of these:

- **Typo in username, password, or portal URL**  
- **Clock wrong on the device** (some apps care about time sync)  
- **Weak Wi-Fi** to the TV (try Ethernet or move the router)  
- **Old app build** (update or reinstall from the provider’s instructions)  

Send a clear message to support with **device model**, **app name**, and a **screenshot** of the error— you will get a faster fix.
